AGRISENSE PLANT DISEASE TREATMENT GUIDE
Source: USDA Extension / UC IPM Integrated Pest Management Guidelines
========================================================

TOMATO — EARLY BLIGHT (Alternaria solani)
Symptoms: Dark brown spots with concentric rings on lower leaves first.
Chemical: Copper hydroxide 2.5g/L or Chlorothalonil 4ml/L every 7 days for 3 weeks.
Organic: Neem oil 5ml/L with dish soap every 5-7 days in evening.
Immediate: Remove infected lower leaves. Do not compost.
Prevention: Crop rotation (3 years). Mulch base. Water at roots only.

TOMATO — LATE BLIGHT (Phytophthora infestans)
Symptoms: Greasy gray-green spots on leaves, white mold on undersides. Spreads rapidly.
Chemical: Mancozeb 2g/L or metalaxyl-based fungicide every 5-7 days. URGENT — spreads fast.
Organic: Copper sulfate 3g/L every 5 days. Remove all infected plants immediately.
Immediate: Isolate infected plants from others today. Apply fungicide immediately.
Prevention: Resistant varieties. Avoid overhead watering. Space plants for airflow.

TOMATO — BACTERIAL SPOT (Xanthomonas campestris)
Symptoms: Small dark water-soaked spots on leaves and fruit with yellow halo.
Chemical: Copper-based bactericide 2.5g/L plus Mancozeb 1.5g/L every 7-10 days.
Organic: Copper sulfate 2g/L spray. Remove infected leaves.
Immediate: Remove infected plant parts. Avoid working with wet plants.
Prevention: Use certified disease-free seed. Avoid overhead irrigation.

TOMATO — LEAF MOLD (Passalora fulva)
Symptoms: Pale green or yellow spots on upper leaf surface, olive-green mold beneath.
Chemical: Chlorothalonil 4ml/L or copper fungicide every 10 days.
Organic: Bacillus subtilis-based spray (Serenade) per label instructions.
Immediate: Improve ventilation. Remove heavily infected leaves.
Prevention: Reduce humidity. Space plants. Use resistant varieties.

TOMATO — SEPTORIA LEAF SPOT (Septoria lycopersici)
Symptoms: Circular spots with dark borders and light centers on lower leaves.
Chemical: Mancozeb 2g/L or copper hydroxide 2.5g/L every 7-10 days.
Organic: Copper-based spray every 7 days. Neem oil as preventive.
Immediate: Remove infected lower leaves. Mulch to prevent soil splash.
Prevention: Crop rotation 2-3 years. Stake plants for airflow.

TOMATO — SPIDER MITES (Tetranychus urticae)
Symptoms: Yellow stippling on leaves, fine webbing on undersides. Leaves bronze, drop.
Chemical: Miticide (abamectin) per label. Horticultural oil 10ml/L.
Organic: Insecticidal soap 5ml/L. Strong water spray to dislodge mites.
Immediate: Spray undersides of leaves with water. Introduce predatory mites if available.
Prevention: Avoid drought stress. Avoid dusty conditions. Monitor regularly.

TOMATO — TARGET SPOT (Corynespora cassiicola)
Symptoms: Brown circular lesions with concentric rings and yellow halo on leaves and fruit.
Chemical: Azoxystrobin or pyraclostrobin fungicide per label every 10-14 days.
Organic: Copper-based fungicide 2.5g/L every 7-10 days.
Immediate: Remove infected plant material. Improve air circulation.
Prevention: Crop rotation. Resistant varieties. Avoid excessive nitrogen.

TOMATO — YELLOW LEAF CURL VIRUS (TYLCV)
Symptoms: Upward curling of leaves, yellowing, stunted growth. No cure.
Chemical: Control whitefly vector — imidacloprid per label or insecticidal soap.
Organic: Reflective mulch to deter whiteflies. Yellow sticky traps.
Immediate: Remove and destroy infected plants to prevent spread. Cannot be cured.
Prevention: Resistant varieties. Control whitefly populations. Use row covers.

TOMATO — MOSAIC VIRUS (TMV/ToMV)
Symptoms: Mottled light and dark green on leaves, distorted growth, reduced yield. No cure.
Chemical: No chemical cure. Control aphid vectors with insecticidal soap 5ml/L.
Organic: Remove infected plants. Wash hands/tools with soap between plants.
Immediate: Remove infected plants immediately. Disinfect all tools.
Prevention: Resistant varieties. Wash hands after handling tobacco products.

APPLE — APPLE SCAB (Venturia inaequalis)
Symptoms: Olive-green to brown velvety spots on leaves and fruit. Fruit cracks and deforms.
Chemical: Captan 2g/L or myclobutanil 0.5ml/L from bud break every 7-10 days.
Organic: Sulfur spray 3g/L. Lime sulfur during dormancy.
Immediate: Remove fallen leaves. Apply fungicide at first sign.
Prevention: Resistant varieties. Rake and destroy fallen leaves. Prune for airflow.

APPLE — BLACK ROT (Botryosphaeria obtusa)
Symptoms: Brown leaf spots with purple margins. Fruit rots starting at calyx end.
Chemical: Captan or thiophanate-methyl per label from petal fall through summer.
Organic: Copper fungicide 2.5g/L. Remove mummified fruit.
Immediate: Prune out infected branches 15cm below visible infection. Remove mummified fruit.
Prevention: Remove dead wood. Avoid wounding trees. Maintain tree vigor.

APPLE — CEDAR APPLE RUST (Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae)
Symptoms: Bright orange-yellow spots on upper leaf surface. Tube-like structures beneath.
Chemical: Myclobutanil 0.5ml/L or propiconazole starting at pink bud stage every 7-10 days.
Organic: Sulfur spray 3g/L during critical infection periods in spring.
Immediate: Apply fungicide at first sign. Remove nearby juniper/cedar galls if possible.
Prevention: Plant resistant varieties. Remove alternate hosts (cedars) within 300m if feasible.

GRAPE — BLACK ROT (Guignardia bidwellii)
Symptoms: Brown leaf spots with dark borders. Fruit shrivels to hard black mummies.
Chemical: Myclobutanil or mancozeb 2g/L from bud break through veraison every 7-14 days.
Organic: Copper sulfate 3g/L. Remove mummified berries.
Immediate: Remove all mummified fruit. Apply fungicide immediately.
Prevention: Remove all mummified berries in winter. Prune for airflow. Avoid overhead irrigation.

GRAPE — ESCA (Black Measles)
Symptoms: Tiger-stripe patterns on leaves, berries with dark spots (measles). Wood decay.
Chemical: No effective chemical cure. Potassium arsenite (restricted use) in some regions.
Organic: Pruning wound protectants. Remove infected wood.
Immediate: Prune out infected canes. Paint pruning wounds with wound protectant.
Prevention: Prune during dry weather. Protect pruning wounds immediately. Avoid large pruning cuts.

GRAPE — LEAF BLIGHT (Isariopsis Leaf Spot)
Symptoms: Angular brown spots on leaves, often following veins. Premature defoliation.
Chemical: Mancozeb 2g/L or copper fungicide every 10-14 days.
Organic: Copper hydroxide 2g/L. Improve canopy airflow.
Immediate: Remove infected leaves. Apply protective fungicide.
Prevention: Train vines for airflow. Avoid excessive nitrogen. Remove infected debris.

POTATO — EARLY BLIGHT (Alternaria solani)
Symptoms: Dark brown target-like spots on older leaves first. Yellowing around spots.
Chemical: Chlorothalonil 4ml/L or mancozeb 2g/L every 7-10 days.
Organic: Copper hydroxide 2.5g/L every 7 days. Remove infected lower leaves.
Immediate: Remove severely infected lower leaves. Apply fungicide.
Prevention: Use certified seed potatoes. Crop rotation 3 years. Maintain plant vigor.

POTATO — LATE BLIGHT (Phytophthora infestans)
Symptoms: Water-soaked spots turning brown with white mold. Spreads extremely rapidly.
Chemical: Metalaxyl + mancozeb or cymoxanil every 5-7 days. URGENT — can destroy crop in days.
Organic: Copper sulfate 3g/L every 5 days. Bordeaux mixture.
Immediate: Apply fungicide TODAY. Remove and destroy infected plants. Do not compost.
Prevention: Certified seed. Resistant varieties. Avoid overhead irrigation. Monitor daily.

CORN — GRAY LEAF SPOT (Cercospora zeae-maydis)
Symptoms: Rectangular gray-tan lesions parallel to leaf veins. Leaves die from bottom up.
Chemical: Strobilurin or triazole fungicide at tasseling if disease pressure high.
Organic: Crop rotation is primary management. Tillage to reduce residue.
Immediate: Scout field regularly. Apply fungicide if lesions reach third leaf below ear.
Prevention: Resistant hybrids. Crop rotation with non-host crops. Reduce crop residue.

CORN — COMMON RUST (Puccinia sorghi)
Symptoms: Brick-red oval pustules on both leaf surfaces. Leaves yellow and die.
Chemical: Triazole fungicide (propiconazole) at first sign if susceptible hybrid.
Organic: Sulfur spray 3g/L as protectant. Plant resistant varieties.
Immediate: Scout for pustules. Apply fungicide if conditions favor spread (cool, humid).
Prevention: Resistant hybrids. Early planting. Monitor regional rust forecasts.

CORN — NORTHERN LEAF BLIGHT (Exserohilum turcicum)
Symptoms: Long cigar-shaped gray-green lesions on leaves. Can cause significant yield loss.
Chemical: Azoxystrobin or propiconazole at VT/R1 stage if disease present and conditions favorable.
Organic: Crop rotation. Resistant hybrids are most effective management.
Immediate: Scout field. Apply fungicide before disease reaches ear leaf.
Prevention: Resistant hybrids (most effective). Crop rotation. Tillage to reduce residue.

PEACH — BACTERIAL SPOT (Xanthomonas arboricola)
Symptoms: Small dark spots on leaves with yellow halo. Spots on fruit. Premature leaf drop.
Chemical: Copper hydroxide 2.5g/L from shuck split through harvest every 7-10 days.
Organic: Copper sulfate 2g/L. Kaolin clay as protective barrier on fruit.
Immediate: Apply copper spray. Remove infected leaves if heavy infection.
Prevention: Resistant varieties. Avoid overhead irrigation. Prune for airflow.

PEPPER — BACTERIAL SPOT (Xanthomonas campestris)
Symptoms: Water-soaked spots on leaves, stems and fruit. Spots turn brown with yellow halo.
Chemical: Copper hydroxide 2.5g/L plus mancozeb 1.5g/L every 5-7 days.
Organic: Copper-based spray. Remove infected plant material.
Immediate: Remove infected leaves. Apply copper spray. Avoid working with wet plants.
Prevention: Certified disease-free transplants. Crop rotation. Drip irrigation.

STRAWBERRY — LEAF SCORCH (Diplocarpon earlianum)
Symptoms: Small purple to red spots on leaves expanding with gray centers. Leaves turn brown.
Chemical: Captan 2g/L or myclobutanil 0.5ml/L every 10-14 days.
Organic: Remove infected leaves. Copper-based spray as protectant.
Immediate: Remove heavily infected leaves. Improve airflow through bed renovation.
Prevention: Resistant varieties. Avoid overhead irrigation. Renovate beds after harvest.

CHERRY — POWDERY MILDEW (Podosphaera clandestina)
Symptoms: White powdery coating on young leaves, shoots and fruit. Leaves curl and distort.
Chemical: Sulfur 3g/L or myclobutanil 0.5ml/L every 7-14 days. Do not apply sulfur above 32°C.
Organic: Potassium bicarbonate 5g/L or neem oil 5ml/L. Improve airflow.
Immediate: Apply fungicide at first sign. Remove heavily infected shoots.
Prevention: Prune for airflow. Avoid excessive nitrogen. Resistant varieties.

SQUASH — POWDERY MILDEW (Podosphaera xanthii)
Symptoms: White powdery spots on upper leaf surface. Spreads rapidly in warm dry conditions.
Chemical: Myclobutanil 0.5ml/L or azoxystrobin every 7-14 days.
Organic: Potassium bicarbonate 5g/L, neem oil 5ml/L, or 1:9 milk:water spray.
Immediate: Remove infected leaves. Apply fungicide or organic spray promptly.
Prevention: Resistant varieties. Adequate plant spacing. Avoid overhead watering.

ORANGE — CITRUS GREENING (Huanglongbing/HLB)
Symptoms: Yellow shoots, asymmetric blotchy mottling on leaves, small lopsided bitter fruit. NO CURE.
Chemical: No cure exists. Control Asian citrus psyllid vector with imidacloprid or spinosad.
Organic: Sticky traps and reflective mulch to reduce psyllid populations.
Immediate: Report to local agriculture authority — this is a regulated disease in many states.
Prevention: Certified disease-free trees only. Psyllid monitoring. Quarantine regulations apply.
